You could be a baritone capable of singing in the tenor range, or vice versa. Practically speaking, at least in opera, you are a tenor if you can sing tenor roles, including the top notes. Otherwise you are a baritone.

    Info-box: What are the Main types of Females and Males voice?
  • Females: The soprano: A soprano is a woman, girl, or boy with a highest singing voice. The soprano voice type is generally divided into the coloratura, soubrette, lyric, spinto and dramatic soprano.The typical soprano voice lies between C4 (middle C) and C6 (high C).
  • Females: The mezzo-soprano voice is the middle-range voice type for females. It lies between the soprano and contralto ranges, over-lapping both of them. The typical range of this voice is between A3 (the A below middle C) to A5 (two octaves higher).While mezzo-sopranos typically sing secondary roles in operas, notable exceptions include the title role in Bizet's Carmen, Angelina in Rossini's La Cenerentola, and Rosina in Rossini's Barber of Seville. Many 19th-century French-language operas give the leading female role to mezzos, including Béatrice et Bénédict, La damnation de Faust, Don Quichotte, La favorite, Dom Sébastien, Charles VI, Mignon, Samson et Dalila, Les Troyens, and Werther, as well as Carmen.
  • Females: Contralto:The contralto voice is the lowest female voice. A The typical contralto range lies between F3 (the F below middle C) to F5 (the second F above middle C). This deep, dark female voice is the rarest female voice and they are known to sound almost like men on their lowest notes.
  • Males: The tenor is the highest male voice within the modal register. His range(C3–C5) indicated on piano keyboard in green with dot marking middle C (C4). The name "tenor" derives from the Latin word tenere, which means "to hold".Within the tenor voice-type category are seven generally recognized subcategories: leggero tenor, lyric tenor, spinto tenor, dramatic tenor, heldentenor, Mozart tenor, and tenor buffo or spieltenor
  • Males: The baritone voice is the middle-range voice type for males; it lies between the bass and tenor ranges, overlapping both of them. Baritone voice range (A2–A4) indicated on piano keyboard in green with dot marking middle C (C4). Baritone comes from the Italian baritono, with its Greek root word barytonos, "deep-voiced," combining barys, "heavy or deep" and tonos, "tone".
